01 — Who I Am

About

I'm a first year PhD student in Computer Science at Washington University in St. Louis, advised by Dr. Kunal Agrawal and Dr. Angelina Lee. My research interests lie in embedded and distributed systems, parallel systems theory, and concurrent data structures. My current work involves optimizing thread allocations for resource constrained systems and analyzing sequential maintenance heuristics applied in concurrent settings.

Before the PhD, I completed my BS/MS at WashU under Dr. Christopher Gill, where my thesis dealt with deadlock-free resource allocation in concurrent systems modeled as graph structures: read it here. Through my various research and work experiences I've covered many areas of computer science. On top of my current work I've built computer vision models for maritime platforms, optimized IO subsystems for industrial controllers, and explored parallel and asynchronous mechanisms in Rust.
